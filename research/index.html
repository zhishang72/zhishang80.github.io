Zhi Shang

Project: hurricane simulation and forecasting

The coupled regional solver was used in CESM.

The surface flux exchange algorithm was used to solve the coupling between atmosphere and ocean.

In the animation, the hurricane Katrina, which emerged and developed into the Gulf of Mexico from August 27, 2005 to August 30, 2005, was simulated. The life track was fully numerically reproduced. The storm growing from a Category 3 hurricane to a Category 5 hurricane was also captured by the simulation after entering the Gulf of Mexico.



Project: Water agitation in a closed tank

The overInterDyMFoam solver was used in OpenFOAM.

The Chimera (overset) grids technique was use to handle the dynamic mesh. The MULES (multidimensional universal limiter with explicit solution) algorithm was used to solve the VOF (volume of fluid) Riemann equation.

In the animation, the left is under the same direction of rotation and speed of the rotators and the right is under the opposite direction of rotation but the same speed of the rotators.



Project: Multiphase flow in porous media

The multiphase flow solver was developed using C++ for OpenFOAM.

This solver is able to handle gas-oil-water-particle 4 phase flow with VOF (volume of fluid).

In the animation, the porosity is 0.3; the gas was colored by blue; water was colored by red; oil was colored by grey; solid particles were colored by particle sizes.

Project: Particle transport in shale fracture

The MPPICFoam solver was used in OpenFOAM.

The simulation for 3600 seconds (1 hour) is under 8 processors done in 1 day with MP-PIC (multiphase particle in cell) DPM (discrete particle model) techneque.

In the animation, the sand particles with everage 300µm normal distribution (100µm - 500µm) inject into the shale fracture with 1000ft long, 150ft height and 0.1in width. Proppant density is 1000kg/m3 and sand particle density is 2650kg/m3. The flow rate is 40bpm with sand concentration 2.5lb/gal.
